Chatter is the sound of money leaving your tool life budget. When our university's CNC gantry started producing visibly rough surfaces at feed rates above 800 mm/min, we had to decide: buy a stiffer machine, or understand why this one wasn't stiff enough.

## Finding the Mode

Modal FEA on the existing frame showed a bending mode at 47 Hz in the Y-axis cross member — right in the range where our spindle speed and flute count produced excitation.

The problem wasn't the rails. It was the **span between bearing blocks** combined with a flat cross-section that flexed torsionally under cutting forces.

## The Fix

We added triangular ribbing to the cross member and relocated the rail mounting surface 12 mm closer to the centroid. Total added mass: 340 g. No changes to the drive system or spindle.

![Cross member redesign](/images/blog/cnc-detail.svg)

Prototype cuts on 6061-T6 showed:

- Surface roughness (Ra) improved from 3.2 µm to 1.9 µm at the same feed rate
- We could push feed to 1100 mm/min before chatter returned

## What I'd Do Differently

Run FEA earlier in the design process. The original frame was built for speed of assembly, not stiffness per kilogram. Stiffness should be a first-class requirement, not a retrofit.
