Getting sensor fusion to work starts long before you write a single line of SLAM code. The extrinsic transform between your LiDAR and IMU has to be right — not approximately right, but measured and verified.

## The Setup

Our test platform uses a Hokuyo UST-10LX mounted 15 cm above the IMU center. The rotation offset was unknown within a few degrees, which is enough to break scan matching in tight corridors.

## Calibration Procedure

We used a hand-eye calibration approach:

1. Mount the robot on a turntable with known angular increments
2. Record synchronized LiDAR scans and IMU orientations at each position
3. Solve for the rotation matrix using SVD on the correspondence pairs
4. Validate by comparing predicted scan overlap against ground truth

The key insight was that **translational offset matters less than rotational alignment** for 2D LiDAR at our mounting height. We spent two days on translation refinement that moved our localization error by less than 2 mm.

## Results

After calibration, loop closure error dropped from 8% to under 1.5% on our standard test course. The full procedure is documented in our lab wiki.

![Calibration setup diagram](/images/blog/calibration-detail.svg)

## Takeaways

- Measure rotation first, refine translation only if residuals are large
- A turntable is worth building — repeatable motion beats freehand rotation
- Log everything during calibration runs; you'll want to replay them
